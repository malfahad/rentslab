# RentSlab Marketing Brief

## Product Overview

RentSlab is a property operations and rent management platform for landlords and property managers. It combines leasing, billing, collections, maintenance, and reporting in one system, with a public tenant payment experience that connects directly to unit-level payment links.

## One-Line Positioning

Run your rental portfolio from lease to collection in one connected workflow.

## Target Audience

- Small to mid-sized property managers managing multiple buildings and units
- Independent landlords scaling from manual spreadsheets to structured operations
- Finance and operations teams that need clean billing, collections visibility, and auditability

## Core Value Proposition

RentSlab helps teams reduce operational overhead and improve collection consistency by centralizing:

- Property and occupancy management
- Tenant and lease lifecycle workflows
- Invoicing, payments, allocations, and credit handling
- Maintenance and vendor coordination
- Operational and financial reporting

## Key Functional Feature Set

### 1) Portfolio and Property Operations

- Landlord, building, and unit management with linked records
- Occupancy-aware portfolio view across buildings and units
- Dashboard visibility into occupancy, rent roll, and portfolio-level totals

### 2) Tenant and Lease Lifecycle

- Tenant onboarding and profile management
- Lease creation and management with billing cycle, rent, deposit, and date controls
- Service subscription support tied to tenant/lease workflows
- Lease close workflow that preserves historical billing data

### 3) Billing, Invoicing, and Collections

- Invoice creation and lifecycle management
- Invoice line items with detailed billing structure
- Payment recording with method, reference, date, and invoice allocation support
- Credit note workflows linked to invoice balances
- Automated invoice issuance runs, including dry-run mode for safe previews

### 4) Payment Links and Tenant Payment Experience

- Unit-level payment codes and stable payment links
- Public payment page per payment slug for tenant-facing payment attempts
- Link activation/deactivation controls for finance teams
- Payment-attempt tracking for collections follow-up and reconciliation

### 5) Maintenance and Vendor Workflows

- Vendor directory for service providers
- Job order lifecycle management with status tracking
- Job orders linked to buildings/units and related expenses
- Tenant recharge support from job orders into invoice billing lines

### 6) Reporting and Analytics

- Report catalog spanning financial and operational use cases
- Core implemented financial reports including rent roll, income statement, and cash flow
- Dashboard analytics for collection trends, occupancy distribution, and open issues

### 7) Organization, Access, and Security

- Organization-scoped multi-tenant architecture
- Role-based access and share permissions
- Account activation, login, password reset, and session-based user flows

## Differentiators to Emphasize

- **Connected rent operations:** Leasing, billing, maintenance, and reporting are linked instead of fragmented across tools.
- **Collections-first design:** Payment codes, public payment links, allocations, and credit notes support practical receivables management.
- **Actionable finance workflows:** Invoice issuance automation with dry-run mode reduces billing risk.
- **Operational-to-financial continuity:** Job orders can drive billable tenant recharge items.

## Primary Messaging Pillars

1. **Centralize operations:** Manage buildings, tenants, leases, and vendors in one platform.
2. **Strengthen collections:** Move from ad hoc payment tracking to structured payment links and invoice allocation.
3. **Improve decision quality:** Use built-in reporting and dashboard signals for finance and operations actions.
4. **Scale with control:** Role-based organization access supports team growth without losing governance.
